## Teaching Model

Professor Drennen runs a **guest speaker series** as the backbone of MGMT 305. This isn't a lecture-and-exam course — it's built around live conversations with real practitioners. The semester rhythm:

1. **Before each session**: Students read about the upcoming speaker and submit written questions through Canvas
2. **Professor prepares**: Reads all submissions, curates the best into a structured interview sheet
3. **During the session**: Professor moderates a live interview with the guest speaker using student-sourced questions
4. **After the session**: Tracks themes, student engagement, and quality trends across the semester

This is a **Socratic, experiential pedagogy** — students learn management by engaging with real leaders, not from textbooks. The professor is the curator and moderator, not the lecturer.

---

## Pain Points

### Primary: Manual Curation is Crushing

With 20–40 students submitting questions as individual PDF/DOCX files inside a Canvas ZIP export, the professor was spending **hours before every guest speaker session**:

- Downloading and opening each file individually
- Reading every submission
- Mentally grouping questions by theme
- Deciding which are strong enough to ask
- Writing a clean interview sheet by hand

This had to happen **every session** — potentially 10–15 times per semester. The labor was unsustainable, especially given the professor's high quality bar.

### Secondary: No Institutional Memory

Before this tool, each session was a one-off event:

- No record of what themes students cared about across speakers
- No way to see if the same "softball" questions kept recurring
- No ability to track which students were improving vs. disengaging
- No longitudinal view of the class's intellectual development

### Tertiary: Isolation

The interview sheet was trapped on the professor's laptop:

- No way to share prep materials with a co-instructor or TA
- No way to let a department chair see the quality of student engagement
- No way to give the guest speaker a preview of what to expect

## Psychographic Profile

### The Researcher-Teacher

This professor treats their classroom like a **research site** — observing how students think, what they care about, how their inquiry evolves:

- **4-tier question quality ranking**: Tier 1 ("Gold Standard") demands questions that expose tensions and trade-offs. Tier 4 is generic advice ("What's your morning routine?") — barely acceptable as backup. The professor has a clear taxonomy of intellectual rigor.
- **"Underlying tensions"** as a first-class analysis dimension — they want to see where student thinking *contradicts itself*
- **"Blind spots"** analysis — what students *didn't* ask about matters as much as what they did
- **Sentiment breakdown** (aspirational / curious / personal / critical) — not whether students "liked" the speaker, but the *type of intellectual posture* students adopted
- **Growth trajectory tracking** (improving / declining / stable) — measuring whether students are getting sharper over time

### High Standards, Personal Investment

- **Student profiles with "Professor Notes"**: AI generates actionable recommendations like mentorship pairings and engagement strategies. This professor intervenes individually.
- **Drop-off analysis**: Flags students who participated early but disappeared — the professor notices absence, not just presence
- **Participation percentage tiers**: 80%+ is success, 50-80% is warning, below 50% is concerning. These are engagement signals, not grading thresholds.
- **Watchlist**: Students flagged for attention with reasons. The professor catches disengagement before it becomes failure.

### Professional, Not Flashy

- **Dark theme** with premium serif typography (Playfair Display) — an academic tool that takes itself seriously
- **"Guest Speaker Intelligence System"** framing — preparation is strategic, not administrative
- **Clean, functional UI** — no gamification, no emoji, no student-facing features. The tool respects the professor's time and judgment.

## Goals & Motivations

| Goal | How the Tool Addresses It |
|---|---|
| **Run better speaker sessions** | Moderator-ready interview sheet: 10 sections, each with a primary and backup question, ranked by quality |
| **Develop students as thinkers** | Growth trajectory tracking, personality analysis, interest mapping, career direction inference |
| **See the class as a system** | Theme evolution across sessions, quality trend direction, collective sentiment breakdown |
| **Eliminate repetition** | Overlapping themes alert — flags when the same themes recur so the professor can freshen the angle |
| **Save preparation time** | Hours of manual curation → seconds of ZIP upload, with better output than manual curation could produce |
| **Share selectively** | Revocable read-only links — share with a department chair or co-instructor while maintaining control |

---

## Decision-Making Criteria

1. **Output quality over speed** — The AI prompt enforces a 4-tier ranking, 10 sections, attribution rules, and backup questions. A tool that produced mediocre output faster would be rejected. The output goes in front of guest speakers (CEOs, executives, industry leaders).

2. **Must fit the existing workflow** — Canvas ZIP export → upload → download PDF/DOCX. No new LMS integration, no student-facing changes, no IT department involvement. Drop-in replacement for the manual process.

3. **Analytics create stickiness** — The core sale is "upload ZIP, get interview sheet." But once the professor sees student growth trajectories and theme evolution, the tool becomes indispensable.

4. **Privacy is non-negotiable** — No self-signup, no public access by default, student names abbreviated to "FirstName L.", sessions are immutable, sharing is opt-in and revocable. FERPA-aware by design.

5. **Must look professional** — The exported PDF goes in front of guest speakers. A sloppy document reflects poorly on the professor and the university.

## Anti-Persona (Who This Is NOT)

- **Not a technophobe** — Adopted a custom AI tool, not a spreadsheet
- **Not a content-delivery lecturer** — The entire pedagogy is interactive
- **Not grade-obsessed** — No grading features; this is about engagement quality, not compliance
- **Not casual about preparation** — The tool's purpose is *better prep*, not less prep
- **Not a mass-market user** — Bespoke tool for one professor's specific methodology; would reject a generic "classroom AI" product

## Buying Trigger

A semester where the guest speaker lineup was particularly dense, and the manual curation process became physically impossible to sustain at the quality level the professor demands. Nothing off-the-shelf existed for this exact workflow — the tool had to be custom-built.
